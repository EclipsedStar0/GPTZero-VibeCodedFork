Elena pulls the kettle from its cradle with a sluggish hand, heat cascading down the ceramic handle and into her own hand-- warm, but not hot enough to burn, the water having been left to sit long enough to lose much of its heat to evaporation. Holding the kettle atop the counter, raised just so, Elena pours the water into the waiting cup, the stream flooding the cup and submerging the solitary teabag within. Then, she lowers the kettle, raising the spout skywards to avoid the cup overflowing, and sets the kettle down. 

And then, she waits.

Her hands steepled and minutes passing, Elena's world becomes naught but the cup of tea before her until some indescribable moment arrives.

With her hands free, she grasps her cup with both hands and raises it yonder, tilting it until the first trickles of tea drip into her mouth-- her tongue poised and ready to retract at the slightest of heat, but she finds her wariness unfounded, for the tea has since cooled after being left to steep. With newfound cognizance of the taste of tea and assurity that her tongue will not be scorched by the heat of the tea, Elena takes a greedy gulp-- nearly draining the cup in one go, uncaring of the tea bag still within until finally, she has to come up for air.
